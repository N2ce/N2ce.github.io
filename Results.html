SAFETY HUMAN PRACTICES MODELING

Results

At the date of writing (18 Oct 2021), there are no successful results. By screening colonies with colony PCR, we discovered that all the transformants have taken up the plasmid backbone only. Hence, no E. coli have taken up the recombinant plasmid. The following are the suspected reasons as to why there are no successful transformants so far.

Incomplete digestion of plasmid and insert

During the design phase, we planned to use two restriction sites, namely SalI and SacI for the creation of sticky ends so that we can insert the targeted gene. However, this also means both restriction sites will have to be digested so that the insert can be successfully inserted into the plasmid. This also means there will be a lower chance of obtaining the desired plasmid as the chance of both restriction sites being digested on both fragments is very small. This reduces the chance of producing the targeted plasmid we want.

Junk DNA in the ordered DNA

By utilizing gel electrophoresis, we discovered that some digested DNA fragments produced smears instead of a clear band that we would have expected. This means there is a lot of undesired DNA in the digestion product, which interferes with the ligation process. It is suspected that due to the complexity of the spidroin sequence, the synthesis of our inserts may not be complete and those incomplete fragments are showing up as smears in our gels.
